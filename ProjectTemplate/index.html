Pascal

Changing the Face of Employee Engagement

When people are asked to do surveys, the reaction ranges from indifference to disdain. Yearly surveys by companies are often done in a rushed, careless way because employees just simply do not want to do them. The data gathered from company surveys serve as a snapshot in time, and are potentially biased by the mood and feeling of the employee on that particular day. Flawed data leads to flawed decisions, which perpetuates an endless cycle leaving neither employer or employee satisfied. Pascal is the answer to the timeless question of, “How do we make surveys less terrible?”

Based on the adorable housecat by the same name, Pascal seeks to change the way that employers engage with their employees. Rather than long and grueling surveys, employees chat with Pascal to create confidential dialog that captures a deeper level of understanding of the feelings of employees. Employers are able to create dialogs that focus on key questions regarding their companies and can use that data to create meaningful change. After employees are done conversing with Pascal, they are able to see the feedback that their peers have given and react to it giving employers a chance to see how widespread complements or complaints are.

Pascal is designed to converse with employees in short, frequent intervals allowing employees to mitigate biases from recent events. To ensure engagement, employers can set rewards for participation. By allowing employers to truly understand their employees, we believe that Pascal can make companies better.
